Project Overview

In Spring 2023, I was awarded the Stipendium Hungaricum Scholarship and an International Mobility Scholarship to complete an academic mobility pathway at the University of Debrecen. After finishing my undergraduate curriculum, I formally coordinated with both institutions to enroll in MSc-level coursework with lecturer approval.

I completed graduate courses in Geoinformatics (Remote Sensing with Uncrewed Aerial Vehicles, Models in GIS, and Hyperspectral Remote Sensing) and Environmental Science (Environmental Policy and Communication), earning a 4.0 cGPA with Excellent evaluations. This training strengthened my integration of GIS, remote sensing, informatics, and biodiversity monitoring.

I also joined a research internship at the Institute of Ecology under PhD candidate Ivan Gonzalez, focusing on phenotypic responses of common blackbirds to urbanization. My field and lab contributions included mist netting, bird banding, radio-tagging, biometric data collection, and support for long-term nesting and capture–recapture analyses comparing urban and old-growth forest populations.
