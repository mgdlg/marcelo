Urbanization Index & Blackbird Phenotypes

Research Intern & GIS Analyst · Institute of Biology and Ecology, University of Debrecen · 2025

In this project, I integrated geospatial analysis into an Urbanization Index used to study phenotypic variation in Turdus merula (Eurasian blackbird) across an urban–rural gradient. The goal was to quantify how urban structure shapes body condition and morphology, and to relate these patterns to landscape composition around sampling sites.

I constructed spatial layers describing land cover, green space, building density, and fragmentation around each sampling point. These variables were then combined into an Urbanization Index, which we linked to biometric metrics such as body mass, tarsus length, wing measurements, and feather condition. Standardized capture and ringing protocols ensured that field data from mist netting and banding could be rigorously compared between sites.

The refined Urbanization Index markedly improved our ability to discriminate between urban, peri-urban, and rural contexts. This allowed us to test hypotheses about how urbanization-driven changes in habitat structure affect bird condition, survival, and potential local adaptation.

Riparian Forests & Urban Bird Diversity

Researcher & GIS Analyst · Lab of Conservation Biology and Sustainable Development – UANL · 2023–2024

For the Santa Catarina River in Monterrey, I studied how riparian forest structure within a heavily modified urban corridor influences bird diversity and community composition. Following extreme disturbance events, this riverbed has evolved into a mosaic of successional habitats, providing an opportunity to evaluate how vegetation recovery affects avian communities.

I processed multispectral satellite imagery to derive vegetation indices (e.g. NDVI) and to map land cover classes along the river. These spatial layers were paired with standardized point-count surveys to model richness and functional diversity as a function of canopy cover, patch connectivity, and surrounding urbanization.

The analysis highlighted riparian segments where regenerating vegetation disproportionately supports bird diversity, providing an evidence base for maintaining or restoring forested strips as part of urban planning and disaster recovery strategies.

Remote Sensing for Reforestation Monitoring

Ecological Data Analyst & Surveyor · Enel Green Power · 2024–2025

At Enel Green Power, I supported reforestation monitoring for renewable energy projects by combining field surveys with satellite-based vegetation analysis. A major challenge was turning heterogeneous, site-level observations into analysis-ready data that could be compared across multiple projects and years.

I built reproducible pipelines in R to clean and standardize field data from large mammal and endangered species surveys, reducing processing times from several days to a single day. These datasets were then aligned with remote sensing products tracking vegetation cover, growth dynamics, and habitat condition within compensation and restoration areas.

The result was a suite of indicators that allowed environmental teams to evaluate whether commitments on reforestation and biodiversity were being met, and to prioritize sites where additional interventions might be needed.

Drone Photogrammetry for Environmental Compensation

Project Manager · IRCA Consultants · 2025–Present

As project manager at IRCA Consultants, I design and oversee drone-based photogrammetry and GIS workflows for environmental compensation projects. These projects often involve monitoring forest cover, erosion, and soil recovery following infrastructure development.

Using UAV imagery, I generate orthomosaics and digital surface models that provide high-resolution baselines for tracking structural changes in vegetation and terrain. I then implement ModelBuilder and Python-based tools in ArcGIS Pro to segment forest patches, derive canopy metrics, and quantify change over time.

Combining this with regulatory requirements and on-the-ground observations allows us to document compliance, identify areas that lag behind restoration targets, and recommend actionable measures to improve ecological outcomes.

NDVI & NWI for Housing Development Planning

GIS Technician & Surveyor · Javer Housing Developer · 2024

For a housing development project, I carried out a historical vegetation and surface water analysis using NDVI and NWI. The aim was to understand how vegetation and wetland-like conditions had changed over time within and around the proposed development footprint.

I processed multispectral imagery from multiple dates, generating NDVI and NWI layers to highlight persistent vegetation, ephemeral water bodies, and areas sensitive to disturbance. These were integrated with field visits, interviews with local residents, and observations of flora and fauna.

The final maps informed layout decisions, helping to avoid the most sensitive zones, maintain drainage patterns, and incorporate green spaces that retain some of the site’s original ecological functions.

Urban Park Biodiversity Monitoring

Biodiversity Monitoring Intern · San Pedro + Parques · 2024

In San Pedro Garza García, I helped standardize and analyze biodiversity monitoring efforts across a network of urban parks. Each park collected data using slightly different methods, making it challenging to compare biodiversity indicators across the system.

I worked with the ecology team to harmonize survey protocols and to design a set of biodiversity metrics that could be computed consistently, including species richness, relative abundance, and functional group composition. Spatial data on park structure and connectivity provided additional context for interpreting these indicators.

This work contributed to a monitoring framework aligned with urban sustainability goals and SDG 11, giving decision-makers clearer signals about where management actions have the greatest ecological payoff.
