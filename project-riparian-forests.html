Riparian Forests & Urban Bird Diversity

Multispectral remote sensing and biodiversity surveys to understand how riparian forests shape urban bird communities.

  • Organization: Lab of Conservation Biology & Sustainable Development — UANL
  • Role: Researcher & GIS analyst
  • Year: 2024

Project Overview

I combined multispectral remote sensing, land cover data, and point-count bird surveys to quantify how riparian forests along an urban river influence bird diversity and community structure. The work supported post-storm environmental planning after tropical storm Alberto and informed public-facing storytelling.

Key Contributions

  • Mapped vegetation structure and coverage along riparian corridors using satellite imagery.
  • Integrated bird survey observations with spatial layers to model biodiversity responses.
  • Presented findings at the DATATON panel and shared insights in a local news segment.
