Project Overview

DATATON is a technical panel organized by Consejo NL, SELUB, Tec de Monterrey, and other institutions. GIS specialists are invited and later selected to participate, bringing spatial analysis into discussions about environmental planning, risk, and public decision-making in Nuevo León.

My first participation was in DATATON 2024, the first edition. That experience is documented in detail in the Riparian Forests & Urban Bird Diversity case study, so this page treats it as the starting point: I presented work connecting multispectral remote sensing, riparian vegetation, and bird diversity along the Santa Catarina River corridor.

My second participation was in DATATON 2026, the third edition, where the group focused on environmental and social risk in the city. The working group was divided into three teams—fires, floods, and soil erosion—and I contributed to the fires team.

For this edition, I developed a Google Earth Engine project to retrieve and analyze city-scale imagery through indices and variables relevant to environmental risk: Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI), Land Surface Temperature (LST), and Normalized Difference Moisture Index (NDMI). The workflow helped the fires team compare vegetation condition, heat patterns, and moisture stress across Monterrey.

I also built an ArcGIS StoryMap for the fires team, translating the spatial analysis into a narrative format that summarized the findings, explained the risk context, and made the team’s outputs easier to communicate beyond the technical working session.

Together, the 2024 and 2026 experiences show an evolution in my DATATON work: from documenting ecological value in the Río Santa Catarina corridor to applying remote sensing and cartographic storytelling to broader environmental and social risk questions in Monterrey.
